MY ADVOCACY

In todays generation children are rapidly investing time on computers nowadays, and the internet has become a regular part of their educational and leisure experiences. This growing reliance on technology means that at any point or another, most kids will have to deal with the problem of cyberbullying, either as a target. Since cyberbullying takes place in cyberspace, bullying online feels permanent. They sense like they are vulnerable, humiliated and overwhelmed. When cyberbullying happens, it is easy to exchange nasty blogs, tweets, or texts with multitudes of people. Our campaign is all about spreading awareness to other people about what cyberbullying can do to affect your mental health. We should always Think before you post when posting or commenting online consider what you say and what effect it may have. Never post comments that are abusive, threatening or are likely to cause offence to others. Come and join us in our campaign about what cyberbullying can do to affect your mental health and how we can prevent it
